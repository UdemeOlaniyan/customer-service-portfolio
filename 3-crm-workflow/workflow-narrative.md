# 🧭 CRM Workflow Narrative — Billing Complaint: Duplicate Charge

**Scenario:** A customer calls in frustrated. They've been charged twice for their monthly subscription and want a refund immediately.

---

## Stage 1 — First Contact (0:00–0:45)

The phone rings. Before I even say hello, I've opened the CRM and my search bar is ready.

**What I say:**
> "Thank you for calling support, this is Udeme. How can I help you today?"

The customer explains the issue — they're frustrated and speaking quickly. I let them finish without interrupting. While they talk, I'm typing their name into the CRM search.

**What I'm doing in the background:**
- Searching their account before they finish explaining
- Noting the emotional tone: frustrated but not aggressive
- Flagging this as likely a billing issue → billing category in CRM

**What I'm thinking:**
- Is this a known billing error affecting multiple customers? (Check system notes)
- Do I have the access to resolve this, or will I need billing team?
- What does this customer actually need: a refund, an explanation, or both?

---

## Stage 2 — Identity Verification (0:45–2:00)

Before I look at anything sensitive, I verify.

**What I say:**
> "I'm really sorry to hear that — let's get this sorted out right now. Before I pull up your account, can I just confirm a couple of details?"

- Full name ✅
- Email address on file ✅
- Last 4 of account ID ✅

**CRM action:** Account located. I can see their billing history.

**Why this step matters:** Even when someone sounds legitimate, skipping verification is a security risk and a policy violation. It also gives the customer a moment to calm down — the process signals that I'm taking them seriously.

---

## Stage 3 — Investigation (2:00–5:00)

I pull up the billing history. I can see it immediately: two identical charges on the same date. This isn't the customer's mistake.

**What I say:**
> "Okay, I can see exactly what happened here. You're right — there are two charges from the same date. I'm going to get that resolved for you right now."

**CRM actions:**
- Document: "Duplicate charge confirmed. Two charges of $[X] on [DATE]."
- Note the charge reference numbers for both transactions
- Check internal notes — is this a known issue? (In this case, no)

**What I'm doing:**
- Confirming the refund process: does this need billing team approval, or can I process it directly?
- If under the auto-approval threshold: I process it myself
- If over the threshold: I prepare the escalation

---

## Stage 4 — Resolution (5:00–8:00)

In this scenario, the refund is within my processing authority.

**What I say:**
> "I've submitted the refund for the duplicate charge — reference number RF-2025-0042. It should appear back in your account within 3 to 5 business days depending on your bank. You'll also get a confirmation email."

**CRM actions:**
- Refund request submitted and reference number logged
- Ticket category: Billing — Duplicate Charge
- Resolution status: Resolved (pending refund confirmation)
- Follow-up flag: Yes — follow up in 5 business days to confirm receipt

---

## Stage 5 — Closing the Interaction (8:00–10:00)

I don't rush to end the call the moment the resolution is handled.

**What I say:**
> "Is there anything else I can help you with today? And again, I apologize for the inconvenience — that shouldn't have happened."

The customer thanks me. The tone has shifted completely from the opening of the call.

**What I say to close:**
> "You're all set. If the refund doesn't appear within 5 business days or you have any other questions, don't hesitate to call back. Have a great day."

---

## Stage 6 — Post-Interaction Documentation (10:00–13:00)

The call is over, but the job isn't done until the ticket is complete.

**CRM documentation:**

```
Ticket ID:          TKT-2025-0318
Contact Type:       Inbound Phone
Issue Category:     Billing — Duplicate Charge
Customer:           [Name] | [Account ID]
Identity Verified:  Yes — name, email, account ID

Issue Description:
Customer contacted regarding two identical charges applied on [DATE].
Charges of $[X] each confirmed in billing history.

Steps Taken:
1. Verified customer identity
2. Pulled billing history — duplicate charge confirmed
3. Checked for known billing issues — none flagged
4. Processed refund within agent authority (under $100 threshold)
5. Provided refund reference number RF-2025-0042
6. Advised 3–5 business day return timeline

Resolution: Resolved — refund submitted
Follow-Up: Yes — 5 business days to confirm receipt
Notes: Customer initially frustrated; resolved positively.
       Consider flagging billing system for duplicate charge pattern review.
```

---

## What Made This Work

1. **Let the customer finish** — they felt heard before anything else happened
2. **Verified quickly without making it feel like an interrogation** — kept it conversational
3. **Confirmed the problem before explaining** — "you're right" matters more than "let me check"
4. **Resolved without escalating** — knew my own authority threshold
5. **Left a follow-up flag** — didn't assume the refund would process without checking
6. **Documented thoroughly** — the next agent or supervisor can see exactly what happened
