# 📞 Phone Call Scripts & Handling Guides

---

## Script 1 — Standard Inbound Call Opening

**Every call starts here.**

> "Thank you for calling [Company] support, this is [Your Name]. How can I help you today?"

**If customer is mid-story when you pick up:**
> "I'm here — please go ahead, I'm listening."

**While they explain:** Open CRM, search their name or account, stay silent except for acknowledgment sounds ("I understand," "okay," "got it") that signal you're listening.

---

## Script 2 — Handling an Angry or Emotional Customer

**The goal in the first 60 seconds is not to solve the problem. It's to make the customer feel heard.**

**Step 1 — Absorb, don't deflect:**
> "I completely understand why you're frustrated — that would frustrate me too. Let me take a look at this right now and figure out exactly what happened."

**Step 2 — Never say "calm down."** Instead:
> "I hear you. Let's work through this together."

**Step 3 — Take ownership even if it's not your fault:**
> "I'm sorry this happened. Whatever the cause, I want to make sure we fix it."

**Step 4 — Redirect to action:**
> "Here's what I'm going to do right now..."

**If the customer becomes verbally abusive:**
> "I want to help you resolve this, and I'm going to do my best to do that. But I do need us to be able to have a respectful conversation. Can we do that?"

If abuse continues after one warning → escalate to supervisor.

---

## Script 3 — Asking Clarifying Questions Without Sounding Like an Interrogation

**Bad:**
> "What's your name? What's your account number? What's the issue?"

**Good — conversational flow:**
> "Let me pull up your account — can I get your email address or the name on the account?"  
*(pause, type)*  
> "Got it — and just to confirm I'm looking at the right account, is your address still [address]?"

The goal is to sound like a person doing their job, not reading from a checklist.

---

## Script 4 — Delivering Bad News

**When you have to tell a customer something they don't want to hear.**

**Lead with the situation, not the policy:**

❌ Bad:
> "Per our policy, refunds are only available within 30 days."

✅ Good:
> "I looked into this carefully, and I want to be honest with you — the purchase date puts this just outside our standard refund window. I know that's not what you were hoping to hear."

**Always follow bad news with what you CAN do:**
> "What I can do is [alternative]. Would that be helpful?"

---

## Script 5 — Setting Hold and Wait Expectations

**Before putting someone on hold:**
> "I need to check on something in our system — can I put you on a brief hold? It should be no more than [X] minutes."

**Never say "just a second"** if it's going to be longer than a second.

**When returning from hold:**
> "Thanks so much for your patience — I have what I need. Here's what I found..."

**If the hold takes longer than expected:** Come back, update them, and ask permission to continue:
> "I appreciate your patience — I'm still working on this. Can I have another [X] minutes? I want to make sure I have the full picture before I get back to you."

---

## Script 6 — Closing the Call

**The close is as important as the open.**

**Standard close:**
> "Is there anything else I can help you with today?"  
*(pause for response)*  
> "Great — you're all set. If anything comes up, don't hesitate to call back. Have a great [day/evening]."

**After a complex issue:**
> "I'll follow up with you on [DATE] to make sure everything has been resolved on your end. Does [email/phone] still work for that?"

**After an escalation:**
> "You'll hear from [team/person] within [timeframe]. Your case number is [ID] — keep that handy. Is there anything else before I let you go?"

## Quick Reference — What To Say When...

| Situation | What To Say |
|-----------|-------------|
| You don't know the answer | "That's a great question — let me find the right answer for you rather than guess." |
| You made a mistake | "You're right, I apologize for that. Let me correct it now." |
| The system is slow | "Bear with me one moment — our system is loading." |
| Customer asks for a supervisor | "Absolutely — let me get someone for you. Can I ask what this is regarding so I can give them the right context?" |
| Customer repeats themselves | "I have that noted — [confirm what you've documented]. I want to make sure I've got it right." |
| You're about to escalate | "I want to make sure you get the right help on this. I'm going to connect you with [team] who specializes in this — and I'll make sure they have everything we've discussed." |
