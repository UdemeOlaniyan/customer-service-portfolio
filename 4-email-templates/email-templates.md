# ✉️ Email Response Templates

---

## Template 1 — Angry or Frustrated Customer

**When to use:** Customer has sent an aggressive or emotionally charged email. They feel unheard or let down.

---

**Subject:** Re: [Original Subject] — We're On It

Hi [First Name],

Thank you for reaching out, and I'm genuinely sorry for the frustration this has caused. You deserve better, and I want to make sure we fix this properly.

I've reviewed your account and can see [brief confirmation of the issue — be specific]. Here's what I'm doing right now:

- [Action 1 — e.g., "I've processed a refund of $X — reference number RF-2025-0042"]
- [Action 2 — e.g., "I've escalated the technical issue to our specialist team"]
- [Action 3 if applicable]

You can expect [specific next step — timeline + what they'll receive].

If there's anything else I can help with, please don't hesitate to reply directly to this email. I'll personally make sure it's handled.

Sincerely,  
[Your Name]  
Customer Support  
[Company Name]

## Template 2 — Billing Dispute

**When to use:** Customer disputes a charge and is requesting a refund or explanation.

---

**Subject:** Re: Billing Question — Your Account Reference [TICKET ID]

Hi [First Name],

Thank you for bringing this to our attention. I've reviewed your billing history and want to give you a clear explanation.

**What I found:**  
[Describe what was found — e.g., "I can confirm that two charges of $X were applied on [DATE]. This was an error on our end."]

**What I've done:**  
I've submitted a refund of $[X] to your original payment method. Your refund reference number is **RF-2025-[XXXX]**. Depending on your bank, this typically appears within 3–5 business days.

If the refund hasn't appeared after 5 business days, please reply to this email and I'll follow up directly with our billing team.

Again, I apologize for the inconvenience. This shouldn't have happened.

Best regards,  
[Your Name]  
Customer Support  
[Company Name]

## Template 3 — Account Access Issue (Password Reset / Locked Account)

**When to use:** Customer cannot access their account and has emailed for help.

---

**Subject:** Re: Account Access — Action Required

Hi [First Name],

I'm sorry you're having trouble accessing your account — let's get that sorted out right away.

I've [unlocked your account / initiated a password reset] from our end. Here's what to do next:

1. Check your inbox (including spam/junk) for an email from [sender address]
2. Click the link inside — it's valid for [X hours]
3. Follow the steps to [set a new password / log in]

If you don't receive the email within 5 minutes, please reply to this message and I'll resend it immediately.

Once you're back in, let me know if everything looks right on your end.

Best,  
[Your Name]  
Customer Support  
[Company Name]

## Template 4 — Follow-Up After Resolution

**When to use:** Proactive follow-up to check that a previously resolved issue is still resolved.

---

Just checking in to make sure everything is working as expected — and that the [refund has appeared / issue hasn't returned / account is fully accessible].

If anything has come up or you have any other questions, feel free to reply here and I'll take care of it.

Hope everything's going smoothly!

Best regards,  
[Your Name]  
Customer Support  
[Company Name]

## Template 5 — Escalation Handoff to Customer

**When to use:** You've escalated a case internally and need to inform the customer.

---

**Subject:** Re: [Original Subject] — Update on Your Case

Hi [First Name],

Thank you for your patience while I looked into this.

I want to be transparent with you: the issue you're experiencing requires specialist attention beyond my immediate access, so I've escalated your case to our [Tier 2 / Billing / Technical] team with full notes — meaning you won't need to repeat yourself.

**What happens next:**  
[Name/Team] will reach out to you via [email/phone] within [timeframe — e.g., "the next 24 hours"]. Your case reference number is **[TICKET ID]** — you can use this if you need to follow up.

I'll also personally check in with you on [DATE] to make sure everything has been resolved.

Thank you for your patience — you're in good hands.

Best regards,  
[Your Name]  
Customer Support  
[Company Name]

## Template 6 — Politely Declining a Request

**When to use:** Customer is requesting something outside policy (e.g., refund past the eligible window, unauthorized account change).

---

**Subject:** Re: Your Request — [Brief Description]

Hi [First Name],

Thank you for getting in touch, and I appreciate your patience while I reviewed your request.

I want to be straightforward with you: unfortunately, [brief explanation — e.g., "our refund window for this plan is 30 days, and your purchase date falls outside that window"]. I understand that's not what you were hoping to hear, and I'm sorry I can't make an exception here.

What I *can* do is [offer an alternative if one exists — e.g., "apply a credit to your account," "connect you with our retention team who may have additional options"].

If you'd like to discuss further, I'm happy to help — just reply here and I'll do my best.

Sincerely,  
[Your Name]  
Customer Support  
[Company Name]
